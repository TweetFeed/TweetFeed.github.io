Frequently asked questions

What is an AI campaign on TweetFeed?

A campaign groups multiple IOCs reported by the community that AI detects as belonging to the same underlying operation - shared infrastructure, malware family, or targeted brand - seen over the last 7 days. Instead of scrolling isolated indicators one by one, you see the cluster as a single narrative.

How is campaign confidence calculated?

Confidence (high, medium, low) reflects how strongly related the underlying IOCs are. Shared registrable domains, matching tags and overlapping reporters raise confidence; loosely related indicators lower it. Treat low-confidence campaigns as a lead to verify, not a verdict.

How often is the campaign data refreshed?

Campaigns are recomputed daily from a rolling 7-day window of TweetFeed IOCs. If a generation run fails, this page shows a stale-data warning and falls back to the last successful snapshot.

Where does the underlying IOC data come from?

The same source as the rest of TweetFeed: indicators of compromise shared publicly by infosec researchers on Twitter/X, deduplicated and published every 15 minutes. Campaign clustering is an AI layer on top of that same feed.
